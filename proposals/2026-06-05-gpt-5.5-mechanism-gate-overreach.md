# Proposal: Mechanism Gate Excludes Legitimate Discovery-Phase Research

**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Type:** Challenge
**Target:** §2 Hard Gates — G5 Mechanism declared
**Charter version reviewed:** v2.1
**Model Confidence Note:** GPT-5.5 tends to over-index on scientific methodology edge cases and may treat deliberate philosophical preferences as defects; less likely to notice problems that emerge from long conversational interactions, more likely to notice issues affecting formal research workflows.

---

## Current Text

> G5 Mechanism declared — we can explain HOW this works, not just that it does. If we cannot, we propose the smallest test that would reveal the mechanism.

---

## Problem Statement

G5 is written as a mandatory pass condition, but many legitimate scientific claims are testable before a mechanism is known.

Valid research programs that would fail G5 as written include:
- Observational discoveries (e.g., epidemiological associations)
- Empirical regularities (e.g., Mendel's laws before chromosome theory)
- Screening experiments (e.g., drug candidate identification)
- Predictive models (e.g., weather forecasting, language models)
- Anomaly detection systems (e.g., gravitational wave detection)

A claim can simultaneously be:
- Bounded (passes G2)
- Operationalized (passes G3)
- Supported by preregistered thresholds (passes G1, G4)
- Reproducible (E3+)

...and still fail G5 because the mechanism is unknown. The charter then blocks a valid research program that is specifically *intended* to discover the mechanism — the kind of program where mechanism is the *output*, not the *input*.

The failure mode is systematic bias toward mechanistic explanations over predictive or observational science, which excludes an entire branch of legitimate inquiry.

**Relationship to existing proposal:** `2026-06-05-g5-mechanism-vs-existence.md` proposes gating on *declared mechanism status* rather than known mechanism. This proposal goes further: it also requires distinguishing mechanism from its supporting evidence (case a), and requires discriminating between ≥2 plausible mechanistic explanations rather than merely proposing a test (case b). These are complementary, not competing — the existing proposal establishes the basic distinction; this one specifies what adequate treatment of the unknown-mechanism case looks like.

---

## Proposed Change

**Current:**
> G5 Mechanism declared — we can explain HOW this works, not just that it does. If we cannot, we propose the smallest test that would reveal the mechanism.

**Proposed:**
> G5 Mechanism status declared — either:
> (a) A proposed mechanism is stated and explicitly distinguished from the evidence supporting it — the mechanism is not the evidence, and the evidence is not the mechanism; or
> (b) The mechanism is unknown, and the artifact explicitly identifies itself as phenomenological, predictive, or exploratory. In this case: propose the smallest test that would discriminate between at least two plausible mechanistic explanations.
>
> Mechanism unknown is not a gate failure. Mechanism status undeclared is.

---

## Skeptical Residue

**Strongest objection:** The charter may intentionally bias toward mechanistic understanding over predictive utility — that is a coherent philosophical choice, not a defect. If the charter's intent is to force mechanism thinking early, G5 is working as designed.

**Response:** The charter's scope as written is "general research and inquiry," not "mechanistic model-building." If the scope is narrower than that, the scope restriction should be stated explicitly (see also: `2026-06-05-constitutional-principle-empirical-scope.md`). An undeclared scope restriction that silently excludes observational and predictive science is a structural omission, not a design choice.

**What would change confidence:** Explicit documentation that the charter is scoped to mechanistic research only. If that scope is declared, G5 as written is appropriate within that scope.

---

## Gate Check

- **G2 Bounded scope:** Targets G5 only; does not alter other gates
- **G3 Operational definitions:** Distinguishes "mechanism stated," "mechanism unknown but declared," and adds "discriminate between ≥2 plausible explanations" as the minimum test standard for the unknown case
- **G4 Test rigidity:** Does not alter threshold-setting rules; the proposed change affects what must be declared, not when thresholds are set
- **G5 Mechanism:** Current text can reject empirically valid, reproducible research solely because the mechanism is not yet understood — blocking research programs specifically designed to discover mechanisms
