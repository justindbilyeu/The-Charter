# Proposal: Session Complexity Trigger Is Not Operationalized

**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Type:** Refinement
**Target:** §9 Session Continuity Protocol — Trigger conditions
**Charter version reviewed:** v2.1
**Model Confidence Note:** GPT-5.5 tends to favor operational definitions and measurable triggers; in some frameworks, intentionally vague guidance may be preferable to rigid criteria.

---

## Current Text

> The assistant produces a State Compression when:
> - Operator issues `/compress`
> - A gate clears or fails
> - A topic shift or milestone is reached
> - The assistant detects rising session complexity
> - The operator requests it at any time for any reason

---

## Problem Statement

Three of the five triggers are operationally clear: `/compress`, gate clears/fails, and operator request. Two are not:

- **"A topic shift or milestone is reached"** — "topic shift" has no definition; "milestone" is not operationalized. Different assistants will interpret these conditions inconsistently.
- **"The assistant detects rising session complexity"** — no measurable indicators, thresholds, or detection procedure are provided.

The failure mode is inconsistent State Compression generation: sessions that need compression don't get it; sessions that don't need it get it arbitrarily. This directly undermines the Session Continuity Protocol's stated goal of treating session state as a first-class artifact — an artifact managed by undefined triggers is not reproducibly managed.

**Relationship to existing proposal:** `2026-06-05-grok-session-complexity-trigger-operationalization.md` addresses the same gap with numeric thresholds (token count, artifact count, topic shift count). This proposal takes a different approach: definitional clarity rather than fixed numerics. The two proposals represent competing solutions to the same confirmed gap — both are filed as data for the human reviewer to weigh. The definitional approach trades precision for flexibility; the numeric approach trades flexibility for precision.

---

## Proposed Change

**Current:**
> - A topic shift or milestone is reached
> - The assistant detects rising session complexity

**Proposed:**
> - A topic shift occurs when the primary claim, artifact, or research objective under active discussion changes to a different claim, artifact, or objective — not when a new aspect of the same claim is explored
> - A milestone is reached when a gate status changes (passes or fails), an artifact is completed and ready for handoff, or a test specification is finalized
> - Session complexity is elevated when two or more active claims, artifacts, or unresolved objections are being tracked simultaneously without a State Compression having been produced since the earliest of them was opened

---

## Skeptical Residue

**Strongest objection:** The charter may intentionally leave trigger discretion to the assistant because conversational structure varies too widely for fixed definitions to apply consistently. A definition of "topic shift" that works for a formal research session may misfire in an exploratory brainstorming session.

**Response:** Inconsistent discretion produces non-reproducible state management, which is specifically what §9 exists to prevent. If the triggers are intentionally discretionary, that should be stated explicitly — not implied by vague language. The proposed definitions can be overridden by the `/compress` and operator-request triggers in cases where the assistant's judgment and the definition diverge.

**What would change confidence:** Evidence that multiple evaluators applying the current text independently reach highly consistent decisions about when State Compression should trigger — i.e., that the vagueness is not producing the inconsistency this proposal assumes.

---

## Gate Check

- **G2 Bounded scope:** Targets the two undefined trigger conditions in §9 only; does not alter the three clear triggers or other sections
- **G3 Operational definitions:** Replaces vague triggers with defined conditions — "primary claim changes," "gate status changes," "two or more simultaneous active claims" — each observable within a session
- **G4 Test rigidity:** No effect on testing thresholds; affects detection conditions, not evaluation criteria
- **G5 Mechanism:** Current text does not provide a reliable mechanism for determining when the trigger fires — two of five triggers have no declared detection procedure, making State Compression generation inconsistent and non-reproducible
