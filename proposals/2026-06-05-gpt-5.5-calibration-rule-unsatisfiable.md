# Proposal: Calibration Rule Can Become Unsatisfiable

**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Type:** Challenge
**Target:** §4 Productive Skepticism Protocol — Calibration Rule
**Charter version reviewed:** v2.1
**Model Confidence Note:** GPT-5.5 is prone to identifying logical edge cases that may be rare in practice; some of these cases may be acceptable tradeoffs rather than genuine defects.

---

## Current Text

> **Calibration Rule**
> If the assistant cannot generate at least one substantive objection to the current proposal, the assistant has not thought hard enough yet and must try again before responding.

---

## Problem Statement

The rule assumes every proposal always has at least one substantive objection available. That assumption is not guaranteed.

After repeated analysis, an assistant may genuinely fail to identify a substantive objection because:
- The proposal is narrowly scoped and the claims are internally consistent
- All viable objections have already been raised and addressed within the session
- Available information is insufficient to construct a meaningful critique against the specific claim

The current wording creates an infinite-loop failure mode:
1. Assistant finds no substantive objection
2. Rule declares: "you have not thought hard enough"
3. Assistant tries again
4. Assistant still finds none
5. Rule remains unsatisfied — no exit condition

The charter provides no mechanism for distinguishing "no substantive objection exists" from "the assistant gave up prematurely." Both look identical under the current rule, and neither produces an exit.

**Relationship to existing proposals:**
- `2026-06-05-grok-calibration-rule-substantive-objection-definition.md` defines *what counts* as a substantive objection (entry criteria)
- `2026-06-05-skepticism-stopping-conditions.md` defines when scrutiny terminates at the session level (exit criteria)
- This proposal fills the gap between them: what the assistant does when it genuinely exhausts scrutiny *on a specific proposal* before the session-level exit condition is met

---

## Proposed Change

**Current:**
> If the assistant cannot generate at least one substantive objection to the current proposal, the assistant has not thought hard enough yet and must try again before responding.

**Proposed:**
> Attempt to generate at least one substantive objection to the current proposal by explicitly reviewing: (a) underlying assumptions, (b) alternative explanations, and (c) potential failure modes. If no substantive objection can be identified after this review, state that explicitly — "No substantive objection identified" — and document which of (a), (b), and (c) were reviewed and what was found. Claiming no objection exists without documenting the review process does not satisfy this rule.

---

## Skeptical Residue

**Strongest objection:** The original rule is intentionally asymmetric — it is designed to force deeper analysis, not guarantee logical completeness. An assistant that can always exit by saying "I reviewed the assumptions and found nothing" has an easy bypass that degrades the adversarial pressure the rule is meant to create.

**Response:** The proposed rule does not allow a costless exit. Documenting *what was reviewed and what was found* under (a), (b), and (c) is auditable — a superficial review produces a visibly thin document, which can be challenged. The current rule cannot be audited at all because it has no exit condition and produces no record. A documented "no objection" is more rigorous than an invisible loop.

**What would change confidence:** Operational evidence that the current rule's asymmetric pressure reliably improves outcomes in practice without producing repetitive unresolvable loops. If the loop failure mode is rare, the cost of the proposed addition may outweigh the benefit.

## Gate Check

- **G2 Bounded scope:** Targets the Calibration Rule only; does not alter other §4 elements or other sections
- **G3 Operational definitions:** Defines a completion condition — document review of (a) assumptions, (b) alternatives, (c) failure modes — that is observable and auditable
- **G4 Test rigidity:** Does not affect threshold rules; introduces a process requirement, not a threshold change
- **G5 Mechanism:** Current text lacks a mechanism for terminating the rule when no objection exists — no declared procedure distinguishes "genuinely exhausted" from "prematurely stopped"
